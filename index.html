OUR STORY

Ryan, Authoy, and Shahriar met in high school. After someone close to Ryan experienced a stroke that could have been much less damaging had it been caught sooner, our founders discussed ways the stroke could have been caught sooner over lunch for months. These conversations soon turned into action. Ryan, Authoy, and Shahriar, with the help of their friends Pari, Deven, and Kevin, leveraged their individual skill sets to create Niura and develop the NeuralOne circuit and conductive earpiece.

Our Mission

Our mission at Niura is to redefine healthcare by harnessing the power of materials science. We are committed to developing innovative solutions that empower healthcare professionals, enhance patient care, and drive medical advancements, ultimately improving the lives of millions.

Impact and Future Vision

Niura envisions a future where our MedTech solutions redefine the standard of care and enable breakthroughs in medical treatments. Frequently Asked Questions

Is Niura’s technology safe for use?

We have integrated our proprietary conductive silicone electrode technology into discreet, sleek earbuds. They are safe for regular use and our technology will be going through clinical trials this summer to determine the scope of their usage. Talk about how we are also HIPPA certified for guaranteed user privacy.

How does Niura differ from other competitors?

Niura produces cutting-edge technology to deliver instantaneous brain monitoring in real-life applications. With its sleek design and ability to deliver common headphone functions, Niura revolutionizes brain-interface software to allow users to monitor their brain health anywhere at any time. Furthermore, we have the opportunity to enhance users’ music-listening experiences. Currently, many individuals rely on random selection or personal preferences when choosing songs to play. Music discovery platforms utilize trends and past listening histories to generate playlists. However, we believe that by integrating EEG electrodes directly into earbuds, we can help people discover new music that empirically benefits the listener's mood, emotions, and energy levels

Is Niura’s technology patented?

Yes, we currently hold 5 provisional USPTO patents regarding different aspects of our product, from the charging dock to our proprietary conductive ear tips.

How can consumers see and interpret the data collected by the earbuds?

We are developing the Niura mobile app so customers can track the data their earbuds collect on the go.

What is Niura’s business model?

We operate around a dual-licensing model, licensing our technology out to companies in both the wireless earbud and biomedical tech industries. The goal of this model is to reach wider audiences, maximize streams of revenue, and ultimately increase accessibility to information regarding brain health for as many people as possible.

What FDA regulations are on your device?

Our device is classified as an FDA Class 2 medical device, presenting a higher category of risk than Class 1 devices as they are more likely to come into direct contact with the consumer. Although there’s control from the FDA on Class 2 medical devices (including postmarket surveillance, patient registries, and guidelines), they are significantly less controlled than Class III medical devices as they do not have a high associated risk.

Why specifically earbuds?

At Niura, we strive to create an effortless and pleasurable experience for our users while monitoring their brain health. By integrating brainwave detection with customizable music options, we ensure that monitoring becomes a comfortable and enjoyable process. Earbuds offer a smaller, subtler package than conventional headphones. Our goal is to make brain health monitoring simple, convenient, and fun for everyone.
